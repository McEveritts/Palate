# 🥔 Roasted Garlic & Brown Butter Pommes Purée 🧄

To serve a braise without a starchy canvas is a culinary misstep; the sauce demands a vehicle. However, standard mashed potatoes are entirely inadequate for the robust complexity of our cider-braised pork. We look instead to the philosophy of the late Joël Robuchon, treating the potato not as a solid, but as a sponge for an emulsion of fat and liquid. By drying the potatoes thoroughly after cooking and incorporating beurre noisette (brown butter) alongside sweet, jammy roasted garlic, we create a silken, luxurious purée that acts as the ultimate supporting actor—rich enough to stand alone, yet designed to catch every drop of the pork’s amber reduction.

### 🛒 Ingredients
*   **Base:** 2 lbs Yukon Gold Potatoes, uniform in size 🥔
*   **Aromatics:** 1 whole head Garlic, top quarter sliced off 🧄
*   **Fat:** 1/2 cup (1 stick) Unsalted Butter, plus 1 tbsp for roasting 🧈
*   **Liquid:** 1/2 cup Heavy Cream, warmed 🥛
*   **Seasoning:** 1 tsp White Pepper, finely ground 🧂
*   **Garnish:** 1 small bunch Fresh Chives, minced finely 🌿

---

### 👨‍🍳 Culinary Execution

I. **Roasting the Garlic**
Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the head of garlic on a square of aluminum foil, drizzle with 1 tablespoon of melted butter and a pinch of salt. Wrap tightly and roast for 45 minutes until the cloves are deeply golden and entirely soft. Squeeze the roasted paste out of the skins and mash it into a smooth puree. **Technique Note:** Roasting garlic transforms its sharp, sulfurous compounds into complex sugars, providing a sweet, umami backbone that elevates the potatoes without overpowering them.

II. **Moisture Management of the Potato**
Place the whole, unpeeled Yukon Gold potatoes in a large pot of heavily salted cold water. Bring to a simmer and cook until tender to the core (about 25-30 minutes). Drain the potatoes. While still hot, hold them with a kitchen towel and peel the skins off using a paring knife. Pass the hot potatoes through a potato ricer back into the empty, warm pot. **Crucial Step:** Place the pot of riced potatoes over ultra-low heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously to evaporate any residual water. Water is the enemy of a velvety emulsion; the drier the potato flour, the more fat it can absorb.

III. **The Beurre Noisette Emulsion**
In a separate light-colored skillet, melt the stick of unsalted butter over medium heat. Allow it to foam and sputter until the milk solids drop to the bottom and turn the color of hazelnut, emitting a nutty aroma. Immediately remove from heat. Fold the warm heavy cream, the roasted garlic paste, and the brown butter into the dried, riced potatoes. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture is cohesive, glossy, and highly elastic. Season with salt and white pepper.

---

### 💡 Chef's Additions & Troubleshooting

*   **Gummy, Gluey Potatoes:** This happens if you use a food processor, blender, or overwork the starch molecules, or if you failed to dry the potatoes after boiling. Always use a ricer or food mill, and gently fold the fats in. If they become slightly dense, fold in an extra splash of hot heavy cream.
*   **Textural Contrast:** Generously blanket the finished purée with microscopic, razor-thin cuts of fresh chives. The sharp, onion-like bite of the raw chives cuts straight through the intense richness of the brown butter and roasted garlic.
