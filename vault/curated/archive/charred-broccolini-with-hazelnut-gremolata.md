# 🥦 Charred Broccolini with Hazelnut Gremolata 🥦

A plate composed of rich pork and buttery potatoes demands an aggressive counterpunch. Charred broccolini acts as our bitter, earthly anchor, while the hazelnut gremolata brings an electric shock of acid and herbaceous freshness. This side dish is an exercise in high-heat manipulation—pushing the brassicas right to the edge of scorching to unlock their natural sugars before shocking them back into balance with raw citrus and garlic. It functions perfectly to cleanse the palate between rich bites of the hero dish.

### 🛒 Ingredients
*   **The Vegetable:** 1 lb fresh broccolini, woody ends trimmed 🥦
*   **The Crunch:** 1/3 cup blanched hazelnuts, roughly chopped 🌰
*   **The Acid:** Zest of 1 lemon, plus 1 tbsp lemon juice 🍋
*   **The Herb:** 1/2 cup flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped 🌿
*   **The Aromatics:** 1 clove garlic, microplaned or minced to a paste 🧄
*   **The Fat:** 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus extra for finishing 🫒

---

### 👨‍🍳 Culinary Execution

I. **The Hazelnut Gremolata Assembly**
In a small, dry skillet over medium heat, toast the chopped hazelnuts until they release their oils and turn golden brown (about 3-4 minutes). Transfer immediately to a mixing bowl to stop the cooking. Add the lemon zest, chopped parsley, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. Toss gently to combine. **Technique Note:** Gremolata relies on the volatile oils of raw garlic and citrus zest. Never add the olive oil or lemon juice to this mixture until the very last second of plating, otherwise the acid will oxidize the parsley, turning it a dull olive green and muting the bright flavors.

II. **The High-Heat Char**
Heat a large carbon steel or cast-iron skillet over high heat. Toss the broccolini in a bowl with 2 tablespoons of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Lay the broccolini flat in the screaming hot pan. Do not move them for 3 solid minutes. Let the intense heat blister and char the stalks. Toss once, charring the other side for 2 minutes. **Crucial Step:** Do not overcrowd the pan. If the pan is too full, the water escaping the broccolini will cause them to steam rather than sear. Work in batches if necessary to ensure aggressive browning.

III. **The Finishing Toss**
Once deeply charred but still retaining a slight bite (al dente), remove the pan from the heat. Squeeze the lemon juice directly over the hot broccolini—it will steam violently—and immediately transfer to a serving platter.

---

### 💡 Chef's Additions & Troubleshooting

*   **Tough Stalks:** If your broccolini has unusually thick stalks, split them down the middle lengthwise before tossing them in oil. This ensures the stalk cooks at the same rate the delicate florets char, preventing a burnt-top, raw-bottom scenario.
*   **Textural Contrast:** Shower the hot broccolini generously with the hazelnut gremolata right at the table. The warmth of the vegetables will gently heat the raw garlic and lemon zest in the gremolata, wafting incredible aromatics into the dining room.
