# 🥔 Skillet-Pressed Pommes Anna 🥔

True culinary mastery often hides behind the fewest ingredients, and Pommes Anna is the ultimate testament to this philosophy. Originating in the 19th-century Parisian culinary scene, this dish relies entirely on the natural starches of the potato acting as a binder, fused together by clarified butter and heat. The result is a striking, golden cake with impossibly crisp edges and a melting, tender center. Served alongside the rich pork chop, it serves as the perfect textural foil, soaking up the Calvados cream sauce while maintaining its architectural crunch.

### 🛒 Ingredients
*   **The Star:** 2 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled 🥔
*   **The Fat:** 6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted and clarified 🧈
*   **The Seasoning:** 1 tsp flaky sea salt 🧂
*   **Aromatics:** 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves, finely chopped 🌿

---

### 👨‍🍳 Culinary Execution

I. **The Slicing and Starch Preservation**
Using a mandoline, slice the peeled potatoes into exact 1/16-inch rounds. Place the slices in a large bowl and toss immediately with the melted clarified butter, salt, and thyme until every slice is coated. **Technique Note:** Unlike French fries, you must never wash or soak potatoes for Pommes Anna. The natural surface starch is the "glue" that will hold the cake together. Washing them will result in a disorganized pile of roasted potatoes rather than a cohesive, sliceable cake.

II. **The Concentric Layering**
Preheat the oven to 400°F. In an 8-inch cast-iron or non-stick oven-safe skillet, arrange the potato slices in overlapping concentric circles, starting from the center and working your way out. Repeat until all potatoes are layered, pressing down firmly on the pile. Place the skillet over medium heat on the stove for 5 minutes until the bottom begins to sizzle and crisp. **Crucial Step:** Cover the potatoes tightly with a piece of parchment paper cut to the size of the skillet, and place a heavy flat weight on top (like a smaller skillet or a foil-wrapped brick) before transferring to the oven.

III. **The Bake and Inversion**
Bake the weighted potatoes for 35 minutes, or until a paring knife glides through the center with zero resistance. Remove the weight and bake for 5 more minutes. Run a spatula around the edge of the pan. Place a flat serving plate over the skillet and, in one swift, confident motion, flip the pan to invert the golden potato cake onto the plate. 

---

### 💡 Chef's Additions & Troubleshooting

*   **Pan Adhesion Paranoia:** The nightmare of Pommes Anna is half the potatoes sticking to the pan during inversion. Ensuring your skillet is properly seasoned (if cast iron) and that you've used enough clarified butter on the bottom layer is vital. If it sticks, gently pry the stuck slices off and patch them back onto the cake like mosaic tiles—no one will know once it's sliced.
*   **Textural Contrast:** Finish the inverted, golden crust with a severe dusting of coarse sea salt right before slicing into wedges. The crystalline crunch against the buttery exterior elevates every bite.
